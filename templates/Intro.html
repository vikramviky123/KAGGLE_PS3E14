Blueberry Yield Prediction

Welcome to Blueberry Yield Prediction

The dataset for this exercise is a blueberry yield prediction dataset, and our goal is to predict the target variable `yield` based on various components and characteristics of the blueberry plants.

Context:

Blueberry yield prediction is essential for optimizing agricultural practices and ensuring a successful harvest. This dataset encompasses information about different aspects of blueberry cultivation, including plant characteristics and environmental factors. Our objective is to predict the blueberry yield based on these features.

The dataset includes the following independent variables:

Independent Variables:

  • clonesize    : Size of the blueberry clone.
  • honeybee    : Presence of honeybees in the vicinity.
  • bumbles    : Presence of bumblebees in the vicinity.
  • andrena    : Presence of andrena bees in the vicinity.
  • osmia    : Presence of osmia bees in the vicinity.
  • MaxOfUpperTRange    : Maximum temperature range in the upper atmosphere.
  • MinOfUpperTRange    : Minimum temperature range in the upper atmosphere.
  • AverageOfUpperTRange    : Average temperature range in the upper atmosphere.
  • MaxOfLowerTRange    : Maximum temperature range in the lower atmosphere.
  • MinOfLowerTRange    : Minimum temperature range in the lower atmosphere.
  • AverageOfLowerTRange    : Average temperature range in the lower atmosphere.
  • RainingDays    : Number of days with rain.
  • AverageRainingDays    : Average number of raining days.
  • fruitset    : Fruit set in the blueberry plants.
  • fruitmass    : Mass of the fruits.
  • seeds    : Number of seeds.

Target Variable:

yield: The target variable representing the blueberry yield.

Metrics:

You can evaluate the model using the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) metric.
